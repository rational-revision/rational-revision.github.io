About the Workshop

This workshop brings together researchers from cognitive science, philosophy, political science, and psychology to explore how, when, and why people update their beliefs—and why these processes so often lead to belief polarisation. By bridging computational, empirical, and theoretical perspectives, we aim to unify diverse explanations of polarisation (ranging from motivated reasoning and cognitive constraints to rational causal differences) and examine their implications for theories of learning, social epistemology, and epistemic trust.
